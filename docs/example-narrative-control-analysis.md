# Worked Example: Narrative Control Analysis

This example focuses on using the Shatter Protocol for deconstructing coordinated narrative efforts in media and public discourse.

## Query
"Examine whether there is evidence of coordinated narrative framing across multiple media outlets and advocacy organizations regarding a high-profile criminal justice policy debate."

## Step 1: Apply Shatter Protocol
Shatter. Shatter.

Key Revelations:
- Multiple outlets and organizations used nearly identical phrasing and framing within a short time window.
- Certain data points and anecdotes were repeatedly emphasized while contradictory data received minimal coverage.
- The dominant framing appeared in both news reporting and advocacy materials with high consistency.

**Revelation:** The appearance of organic, independent coverage may mask coordinated narrative development and distribution.

## Step 2: Structured Analysis
Using mapping and evidence principles:

**Key Observations:**
- Similar headlines and lead paragraphs across different outlets
- Shared sourcing from a small number of advocacy groups and experts
- Coordinated timing of report releases and media coverage

**Evidence Assessment:**
- Timing and phrasing alignment → **INFERRED** (strong patterns)
- Direct coordination between organizations and media → **SPECULATIVE** (plausible but harder to prove without internal documents)

## Final Synthesized Output
There is clear evidence of highly aligned messaging across multiple organizations and media outlets regarding this policy debate. While this does not prove direct collusion, the patterns strongly suggest coordinated narrative development rather than independent journalistic or analytical processes. This type of analysis helps surface potential narrative management that may not be obvious from any single source.

Key Takeaway:
The Shatter Protocol is particularly effective at revealing coordinated narrative efforts that traditional single-source analysis often misses. Combining it with evidence labeling produces more nuanced and defensible public analysis.

# Worked Example: Full Stack Civic Analysis

This example demonstrates using multiple frameworks together on a complex civic research question (Shatter + Sovereign Lens + Evidence Gate).

## Query
"Analyze the influence of a national network of organizations on state-level criminal justice reform efforts, including narrative framing, funding, and policy outcomes."

## Step 1: Apply Shatter Protocol
Shatter. Shatter.

Key Revelations:
- The dominant narrative frames the network as a nonpartisan reformer focused on reducing incarceration.
- There are observable patterns of coordinated messaging, selective data usage, and alignment with certain political and donor interests.
- The “bipartisan reform” framing may obscure deeper ideological or strategic motivations.

## Step 2: Apply Sovereign Lens
Using structured reasoning:

**Scope:** National network’s influence on state criminal justice reform (2015–2025), focusing on funding, narrative, and policy impact.

**Module Activation:**
- Influence Mapping module → Map key organizations, funders, and personnel
- Narrative Analysis module → Examine public messaging and media alignment
- Policy Outcomes module → Review legislative changes and measurable results

## Step 3: Apply Evidence Gate
Major claims labeled:

- Coordinated narrative across multiple states → **INFERRED** (strong timing and content alignment)
- Significant funding from a small set of national foundations → **SOURCED** (public grant data)
- Direct causation between network activity and specific policy changes → **SPECULATIVE** (correlation exists, direct causation is harder to prove)

## Final Synthesized Output
A coordinated national network has played a major role in shaping criminal justice reform debates and legislation across multiple states. While some influence is visible through funding and narrative alignment, claims of direct control over policy outcomes require more evidence. The combination of Shatter (to surface layers), Sovereign Lens (for structured analysis), and Evidence Gate (for disciplined labeling) produces a more balanced and credible assessment than any single framework.

Key Takeaway:
Using frameworks in combination (Shatter for perception, Sovereign Lens for structure, Evidence Gate for rigor) creates significantly stronger civic research than relying on any one tool alone.

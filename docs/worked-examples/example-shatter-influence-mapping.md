# Worked Example: Shatter + Influence Mapping

This example shows how to combine the Shatter Protocol with the Influence & Organizational Mapping Framework.

## Query
"Examine the public narrative around Organization X’s influence in Policy Area Y. Identify key actors, funding relationships, and potential narrative control mechanisms."

## Step 1: Apply Shatter Protocol
Shatter. Shatter.

Key Revelations:
- The dominant public narrative presents Organization X’s involvement as purely expertise-driven and philanthropic.
- There are observable patterns of coordinated messaging across multiple organizations and media outlets.
- Funding flows and personnel overlaps suggest tighter coordination than is publicly acknowledged.

## Step 2: Apply Influence Mapping Framework
Using the mapping template:

**Scope:** Public narrative and influence around Organization X in Policy Area Y (last 5 years).

**Key Entities:** Organization X, aligned nonprofits, major donors, media outlets, government officials.

**Key Relationships:** Multiple funding relationships, personnel movement (revolving door), coordinated public messaging.

**Influence Observations:** Organization X exerts significant influence through funding, personnel placement, and media relationships.

**Disclosure Patterns:** Some funding is disclosed, but full coordination is not transparently documented.

**Confidence Levels:** Funding and personnel overlaps: High. Coordinated narrative control: Medium.

## Final Synthesized Output
Organization X exerts considerable influence over the public narrative in Policy Area Y through funding, personnel placement, and aligned messaging. The dominant narrative that frames its role as purely expertise-based is incomplete.

Key Takeaway:
Shatter surfaced potential narrative distortion. Influence Mapping provided structure to document actors and influence flows.
